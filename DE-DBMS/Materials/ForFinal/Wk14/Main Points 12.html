Lesson 12 - Distributed Databases
 
Infinity at a Point

A distributed database is a collection of multiple, logically interrelated collections of shared data (and a description of this data), physically distributed over a computer network yet appearing as an undifferentiated wholeness to the user.

In Unity consciousness all the diversity in the world is seen to be expressions of ones own Self.

MAIN POINTS

1. A DDBMS reflects the organizational structure, improves reliability, availability and performance, and allows for modular growth. DBAs are increasingly desiring that their systems have the same properties as the Unified field of all the laws of Nature. These properties include infinite correlation, perfect orderliness, frictionless flow of information and infinite flexibility.

2. A relation may be divided into a number of sub-relations called fragments which may be horizontal, vertical, mixed or derived. Although fragmented, the concept of the whole relation is maintained by the DDBMS. Similarly the diversity of the relative field of creation is merely an expression of the wholeness of the unified field of all the laws of Nature.

3.  The DDBMS should appear like a centralized DBMS by providing distribution transparency, transaction transparency, performance transparency and DBMS transparency. The idea here is that the DDBMS, not the user, is doing all the work of organizing the parts into a whole. In a state of enlightenment one's desires are carried out by Nature and fulfillment of one's desires is effortless.

CONNECTING THE PARTS OF KNOWLEDGE WITH THE WHOLENESS OF KNOWLEDGE

1. It is often the case that the data of an enterprise is stored in different locations and databases.

2. The reference architecture for a DDBMS consists of a set of global external schemas, a global conceptual schema, fragmentation schema, and an allocation schema. Also each local DBMS conforms to the ANSI-SPARC three level architecture.
