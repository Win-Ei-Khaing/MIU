Conceptual database design starts with the expected users of the database and creates a user model of the data for each type of user, based on their information requirements.

The TM technique starts at the grossest level of thinking and brings the awareness to subtler and more universal levels.

MAIN POINTS

1. A conceptual model comprises: entity types, relationship types, attributes, attribute domains, candidate keys, and primary keys. Any model provides a set of concepts that assist the user of model in interacting with some aspect of the world for some purpose. Vedic Science uses the concepts of Rishi (knower), Devata (process of knowing), and Chhandas (known) to model the world of change by understanding that the source of the Relative is the non-changing field of pure self-referral consciousness, the Samhita of Rishi, Devata and Chhandas. The purpose of the RDC model is to bring enlightenment.

2. The mapping between the ER model and the target logical model will be straightforward if the logical model is robust enough to handle all the concepts present in the ER model. The relative and absolute fields are both full: two fullnesses in harmony with one another.

3. A logical model is validated by using normalization and by testing it against user transactions. In a science a model must always be tested against the real-world phenomenon that is being modeled. In Vedic Science, experiences during the practice of TM and TM Sidhi programs are validated by observing predicted results during activity.
